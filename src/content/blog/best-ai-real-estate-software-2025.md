## What Is AI Real Estate Software?

AI real estate software uses machine learning, predictive analytics, and large language models (LLMs—AI systems trained on massive text datasets to generate and understand human language) to automate work agents and investors once did by hand. Lead scoring, property valuations, market forecasting, listing descriptions—algorithms trained on millions of real estate data points now handle all of it.

Traditional CRMs store contacts. Standard listing tools display properties. AI-powered platforms go further. They read behavioral signals. They predict which homeowners are likely to sell. They sort your leads automatically. The National Association of Realtors reported that 35% of Realtors used AI tools at least once per month in 2024, up sharply from prior years (Source: NAR Member Profile, 2024).

Key use cases: lead generation, automated property valuation (AVMs—statistical models that estimate a property's market value without a manual appraisal), contract review, and hyperlocal market forecasting. The spread of LLMs through 2024 and into 2025 pushed development forward across all of these areas.

## Lofty (Chime) – Best AI CRM for Agents

Lofty, formerly Chime CRM, is an AI-powered CRM built for real estate professionals. It puts lead scoring, behavioral tracking, and automated follow-up in one place—built around how agents actually work.

The AI is where it pulls ahead. Predictive lead scoring reads website behavior, email engagement, and property search patterns, then ranks leads by conversion likelihood. The built-in AI chatbot talks to website visitors in real time, qualifying prospects before they reach your phone. Smart drip campaigns adjust messaging based on how leads engage with your content.

Pricing starts around $500 per month for teams as of early 2025. Brokerage tiers with IDX websites—sites that display live MLS listings—and advanced reporting cost more. Solo agents may find that steep. Individual plans exist but still run several hundred dollars a month.

**Pros:** Deep MLS integration across most major US markets, a solid mobile app, and documented results. One Phoenix-based team of six agents reported a 22% increase in lead-to-appointment conversion after switching to Lofty's AI scoring in 2024.

**Cons:** Automation setup takes real work. Plan two to three weeks configuring workflows before you get full value. Solo agents on tight budgets should look at PropStream or a standalone CRM paired with a cheaper lead source.

**Best for:** Mid-size teams and brokerages that need a full CRM with AI built in. Agents already using Follow Up Boss who want stronger AI capabilities will find Lofty a natural next step.

## Offrs – Best for Predictive Seller Lead Gen

Offrs uses AI to predict which homeowners in a given area are most likely to sell within 12 months. If your business runs on listings, this tool was built for exactly that problem.

The platform analyzes over 250 data signals—life events, property equity, tax records, ownership duration—to score each homeowner's likelihood of selling. You buy exclusive access to a zip code territory. No other Offrs subscriber in your area gets the same leads. The platform also runs automated marketing campaigns through direct mail and digital ads to reach predicted sellers.

Pricing is territory-based. As of Q1 2025, most plans run $200–$400 per month depending on zip code density and market competition. Most require a 6- to 12-month commitment.

**Pros:** Zip code exclusivity means no direct competition with other Offrs users. The platform connects to major CRMs including Follow Up Boss and Lofty. Offrs reports a prediction accuracy rate of roughly 72% for identifying sellers within 12 months (Source: Offrs, 2024).

**Cons:** Results vary a lot by market. Rural areas with low turnover may not produce enough volume to justify the cost. The long-term commitment locks you in before you can fully judge the ROI. Agents in low-inventory markets often find lead volume disappointing compared to suburban or metro zip codes.

**Real-world example:** A listing agent in suburban Atlanta farmed three zip codes with Offrs and secured seven listings in nine months from predicted seller leads—roughly $42,000 in commission income tied directly to the platform.

**Best for:** Listing-focused agents and teams who want a steady pipeline of seller leads without paying Zillow or Redfin referral fees.

The core product is its AVM—machine learning trained on transaction data, tax records, and property characteristics across more than 100 million US properties. Beyond point-in-time valuations, HouseCanary forecasts price trends up to 36 months out and scores property condition using image analysis.

Pricing is enterprise-level in 2025. Custom quotes for API access and bulk usage. Smaller users can buy individual property reports, but per-report pricing isn't listed publicly—something many users find frustrating, and so do we.

**Pros:** Accuracy is the main selling point. HouseCanary's AVM has a reported median error rate of about 3.2% nationally. For context, Zillow's Zestimate sits at roughly 2.4% median error for on-market homes but jumps to around 7.5% for off-market properties (Source: Zillow, 2024). HouseCanary holds steady across both categories (Source: HouseCanary, 2024). The API is well-documented, so tech-savvy teams building custom tools will find it easy to work with. **Cons:** This is not a tool for agents showing comps at a kitchen table. The interface is built for analysts. Pricing opacity is a real barrier for smaller users who want to test the product before scaling.

**Best for:** Real estate investors managing portfolios, mortgage lenders needing automated appraisal support, and brokerages with in-house data teams.

## How to Choose the Right AI Real Estate Software

Match the tool to your actual business model first. A solo buyer's agent, a 15-person listing team, and a wholesaler flipping 20 properties a year need completely different platforms. Agents who buy software built for a different business tend to cancel within three months.

Calculate cost per lead and cost per closed deal—not just the monthly fee. If Offrs costs $300 per month and generates two closings per quarter at $8,000 commission each, your effective cost per deal is $450. That number matters more than the sticker price. Research on conversion optimization reinforces that the tools closest to the point of conversion—lead scoring, follow-up automation—tend to deliver the highest measurable ROI (Source: Baymard Institute, 2024).

Check integrations before signing anything. Does the tool connect to your existing CRM, your local MLS, and your dialer? Ask vendors specifically which MLS boards they support—coverage varies a lot by region. Some smaller or independent MLS systems aren't supported by any of these platforms.

Ask about data quality. How recently was the training data updated? Does it cover your specific US market, including rural areas if that's where you work? Stale training data is one of the most common reasons AI predictions fall short. Also, prioritize tools that offer free demos, trials, or money-back guarantees. Any vendor confident in their product will let you test it first.

## AI Real Estate Trends to Watch in 2025

Generative AI for listing descriptions and marketing copy has moved from experimental to standard. Tools built on LLMs now produce property descriptions, social media posts, and email campaigns in seconds. Output quality is strong enough that most consumers can't tell AI-generated listings from human-written ones. Most experienced agents still edit for local nuance and brand voice.

AI-powered contract review and compliance tools are developing fast. Several startups—including Propy and SkySlope with its AI features—are building platforms that scan purchase agreements, flag missing disclosures, and check compliance with state-specific rules. These tools cut review time but should not replace legal counsel for complex transactions. Expect major brokerages to adopt them by late 2025.

Voice AI for 24/7 lead response is also gaining ground. Instead of a text chatbot, these tools answer phone calls and hold natural conversations—qualifying leads, answering property questions, booking showings without a human involved. Early results are promising. But callers sometimes disengage when they realize they're talking to AI, especially during high-stakes conversations about pricing.

After the 2024 NAR settlement and its rule changes around commission transparency and data sharing, MLS organizations are increasingly partnering with AI platforms to share richer data feeds. This is enabling hyperlocal price forecasting at the neighborhood level with improving accuracy (Source: T3 Sixty, 2025). ## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best AI software for real estate agents in 2025?
Lofty (formerly Chime) is the top pick for agents and teams who need an AI-powered CRM with lead scoring and automated follow-up. For predictive seller leads specifically, Offrs is a strong alternative. The right choice depends on whether you need a full CRM or targeted lead generation—see our [real estate lead generation software](/real-estate-lead-generation-software) guide for more options.

### Can AI really predict which homeowners will sell?
Yes, with real accuracy. Tools like Offrs and SmartZip analyze 250–300+ data signals—life events, equity levels, ownership duration—to score homeowners likely to list within 12 months. No tool is perfect, and accuracy varies by market density. But these tools typically beat cold prospecting by a wide margin. Think of them as a prioritization layer, not a crystal ball.

### How much does AI real estate software cost?
Costs vary widely as of Q1 2025. PropStream starts at $99/month for investors. Agent CRMs like Lofty run $400–$700/month for teams. Enterprise platforms like HouseCanary and Reonomy require custom quotes that can reach thousands per month depending on usage volume.

### Is AI real estate software worth it for solo agents?
It depends on your budget and transaction volume. PropStream and Offrs offer pricing that can work for solo agents doing at least a few closings per quarter. Avoid enterprise tools priced for teams unless your volume justifies it. A useful benchmark: if the monthly subscription exceeds 5% of your average monthly commission income, you need higher volume before the tool pays for itself.

### Does AI real estate software integrate with the MLS?
Most top platforms—Lofty, BoomTown, Follow Up Boss—offer MLS integrations for listing data syncing. Always confirm your specific MLS board is supported before signing up. Coverage varies by region. Some smaller or independent MLS systems aren't supported.

### What's the difference between an AVM and an AI valuation tool?
An AVM (automated valuation model) uses statistical modeling to estimate property value based on comparable sales and property data. AI valuation tools like HouseCanary layer machine learning on top of that—improving accuracy, factoring in property condition from images, and forecasting future value trends, not just current worth. A traditional AVM gives you a snapshot. An AI-enhanced tool gives you a snapshot plus a directional forecast.
