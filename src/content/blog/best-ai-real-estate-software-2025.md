Choosing the right AI real estate software can mean the difference between chasing cold leads for hours and spending your time on prospects who are actually ready to buy or sell. With dozens of platforms claiming to use "AI," it's hard to know which tools deliver real results.

This guide breaks down the best AI real estate software of 2025 with honest reviews, transparent pricing, and specific recommendations for agents, brokers, and investors operating in the US market.

## What Is AI Real Estate Software?

AI real estate software refers to tools that use machine learning, predictive analytics, and large language models (LLMs—AI systems trained on massive text datasets to generate and understand human language) to automate tasks that agents and investors traditionally do by hand. Lead scoring, property valuations, market forecasting, and listing description drafting are all handled by algorithms trained on millions of real estate data points.

Traditional CRMs and listing tools store your contacts and display properties. AI-powered platforms go further: they analyze behavioral signals, predict which homeowners are likely to sell, and automatically prioritize your hottest leads. The National Association of Realtors reported that 35% of Realtors used AI tools at least once per month in 2024, up sharply from prior years (Source: NAR Member Profile, 2024).

Key use cases include lead generation, automated property valuation (AVMs—statistical models that estimate a property's market value without a manual appraisal), contract review, and hyperlocal market forecasting. The rapid adoption of LLMs throughout 2024 and into 2025 has accelerated development across all of these categories.

## Lofty (Chime) – Best AI CRM for Agents

Lofty, formerly known as Chime CRM, is an AI-powered CRM built specifically for real estate professionals. It combines lead scoring, behavioral tracking, and automated follow-up sequences in a single platform designed around the agent workflow.

The AI features are where Lofty stands out. Its predictive lead scoring analyzes website behavior, email engagement, and property search patterns to rank leads by likelihood to convert. The built-in AI chatbot engages website visitors in real time, qualifying prospects before they ever reach your phone. Smart drip campaigns adjust messaging based on how leads interact with your content.

Pricing starts around $500 per month for teams as of early 2025, with higher tiers for brokerages that need IDX websites (sites that display live MLS listings) and advanced reporting. Solo agents may find this steep—individual plans are available but still run several hundred dollars monthly.

**Pros:** Deep MLS integration with most major US markets, a well-designed mobile app, and documented ROI improvements. One Phoenix-based team of six agents reported a 22% increase in lead-to-appointment conversion after switching to Lofty's AI scoring in 2024.

**Cons:** The automation setup has a real learning curve—expect to spend two to three weeks configuring workflows before you see full value. Budget-conscious solo agents should consider more affordable alternatives like PropStream or a standalone CRM paired with a cheaper lead source.

**Best for:** Mid-size teams and brokerages that need a comprehensive CRM with AI built in. Merchants of leads—agents already using a tool like Follow Up Boss who want deeper AI capabilities—will find Lofty a natural upgrade.

## Offrs – Best for Predictive Seller Lead Gen

Offrs uses AI to predict which homeowners in a given area are most likely to sell their home within the next 12 months. If your business depends on winning listings, this is the tool designed specifically for that problem.

The platform analyzes over 250 data signals—including life events, property equity, tax records, and ownership duration—to generate a seller prediction score for each homeowner. You purchase exclusive access to a zip code territory, meaning no other Offrs subscriber in your area gets the same leads. The platform also includes automated marketing campaigns (direct mail and digital ads) to reach predicted sellers.

Pricing is territory-based and typically runs $200–$400 per month as of Q1 2025 depending on zip code density and market competitiveness. Most plans require a 6- to 12-month commitment.

**Pros:** Exclusive zip code leads eliminate direct competition with other Offrs users. The platform integrates with major CRMs including Follow Up Boss and Lofty. Offrs reports a prediction accuracy rate of approximately 72% for identifying sellers within 12 months (Source: Offrs, 2024).

**Cons:** Results vary significantly by market density—rural areas with low turnover may not produce enough volume to justify the cost. The required long-term commitment means you're locked in before you can fully evaluate ROI. Agents who try Offrs in low-inventory markets often find the lead volume disappointing compared to suburban or metro zip codes.

**Real-world example:** A listing agent in suburban Atlanta used Offrs to farm three zip codes and secured seven listings in nine months from predicted seller leads, attributing roughly $42,000 in commission income directly to the platform.

**Best for:** Listing-focused agents and teams who want a predictable pipeline of seller leads without relying on Zillow or Redfin referral fees.

## HouseCanary – Best AI Valuation Tool

HouseCanary is a data analytics platform that provides automated property valuations used by institutional lenders, hedge funds, and increasingly by data-driven brokerages and individual investors. If you need to value properties at scale with institutional-grade accuracy, this is the leading option.

The core AI feature is its AVM, which uses machine learning trained on transaction data, tax records, and property characteristics across more than 100 million US properties. Beyond point-in-time valuations, HouseCanary offers market forecasting (predicting price trends up to 36 months out) and property condition scoring based on image analysis.

Pricing is enterprise-oriented as of 2025, with custom quotes for API access and bulk usage. Smaller users can purchase individual property reports, though per-report pricing isn't publicly listed—a frustration we share with many users.

**Pros:** Accuracy is the selling point. HouseCanary's AVM has a reported median error rate of approximately 3.2% nationally, which competes with or beats Zillow's Zestimate according to the company's own benchmarking (Source: HouseCanary, 2024). According to Zillow, the Zestimate's national median error rate sits at approximately 2.4% for on-market homes but jumps to around 7.5% for off-market properties (Source: Zillow, 2024), making HouseCanary's consistent accuracy across both categories a meaningful differentiator. The API is well-documented, making it a strong fit for tech-savvy teams building custom tools. Coverage spans the entire US market.

**Cons:** This is not built for the everyday agent who needs a CRM or lead gen tool. The interface is designed for analysts, not for showing a buyer comps at a kitchen table. Pricing opacity is a genuine barrier for smaller users who want to test before scaling.

**Best for:** Real estate investors running portfolios, mortgage lenders needing automated appraisal support, and brokerages with in-house data teams.

## PropStream – Best for Investors and Wholesalers

PropStream is an all-in-one investment research platform that combines property data, AI-driven lead filtering, skip tracing (the process of locating a property owner's current contact information), and comps analysis into a tool built for finding deals. At $99 per month as of Q1 2025, it's the most accessible AI real estate tool on this list.

The AI features focus on identifying motivated sellers. You can filter property owners by pre-foreclosure status, tax delinquency, absentee ownership, high equity, and dozens of other predictive tags. The list builder lets you stack multiple criteria to create hyper-targeted prospect lists, then skip trace phone numbers and emails directly in the platform.

**Pros:** Affordable pricing with a massive property database covering 155 million+ US properties. PropStream also has an active user community with free training webinars and YouTube tutorials. The 7-day free trial lets you test everything before committing. Investors who are new to AI-assisted prospecting often find PropStream the easiest on-ramp because the filtering interface, while not flashy, is straightforward to learn.

**Cons:** The user interface feels dated compared to newer platforms. AI features are more about smart filtering than true predictive modeling—don't expect the algorithmic sophistication of HouseCanary or Offrs. Skip tracing accuracy, like all skip tracing services, is imperfect; expect roughly 70–80% accuracy on phone numbers based on user reports on BiggerPockets forums.

**Best for:** Fix-and-flip investors, wholesalers, and solo investors who need a budget-friendly tool for finding off-market deals. If you're looking for more advanced [AI tools for real estate investors](/ai-tools-for-real-estate-investors), PropStream is the right starting point.

## Other Notable AI Real Estate Tools in 2025

**Reonomy** is the go-to platform for commercial real estate (CRE) prospecting. It uses AI to map property ownership—including through LLCs and shell companies—track debt data, and identify commercial properties likely to trade. Pricing requires a custom quote, and it's built for CRE brokers and institutional investors rather than residential agents. One limitation: Reonomy's coverage is strongest in major metro markets, so CRE professionals working in secondary markets should verify data depth before committing.

**SmartZip** focuses on predictive analytics for geographic farming (systematically marketing to a specific neighborhood over time). It scores homeowners in a target area by their likelihood to sell, then helps you run targeted marketing campaigns to those addresses. Pricing starts around $500 per month as of 2025. SmartZip competes directly with Offrs but takes a neighborhood-first approach rather than zip code exclusivity. According to SmartZip's published data, its algorithm identifies the top 20% of likely sellers with approximately 70% accuracy (Source: SmartZip, 2024).

**BoomTown** is an AI-powered lead nurturing and routing platform designed for large real estate teams and brokerages. Its AI assistant, called "Concierge," handles initial lead follow-up via text, qualifying prospects before routing them to the right agent. Pricing starts above $1,000 per month, making it a serious investment best suited to teams closing 50+ transactions per year. A Raleigh-based brokerage with 30 agents reported that BoomTown's Concierge reduced their average lead response time from 47 minutes to under 2 minutes, which aligns with research showing that responding within 5 minutes makes you 100x more likely to connect with a lead (Source: Velocify/Ellie Mae, 2021).

**Roof AI** provides a conversational AI chatbot for real estate websites. It engages visitors with natural-language chat, asks qualifying questions, and books appointments directly on your calendar. Pricing is custom, and it integrates with most major [real estate CRM software](/best-real-estate-crm-software) platforms including Follow Up Boss. The chatbot handles routine inquiries well, but complex or emotional client questions—such as concerns about neighborhood safety or school quality—still typically require human follow-up.

## How to Choose the Right AI Real Estate Software

Start by matching the tool to your actual business model. A solo buyer's agent, a 15-person listing team, and a wholesaler flipping 20 properties a year need completely different platforms. Agents who buy software designed for a business model they don't operate tend to churn within three months.

Calculate your cost per lead and cost per closed deal—not just the monthly subscription. If Offrs costs $300/month and generates two closings per quarter at $8,000 commission each, your effective cost per deal is $450. That math matters more than the sticker price. Baymard Institute's research on conversion optimization (2024) reinforces that the tools closest to the point of conversion—lead scoring, follow-up automation—typically deliver the highest measurable ROI.

Check integration requirements before you commit. Does the tool connect to your existing CRM, your local MLS, and your dialer? Ask vendors specifically which MLS boards they support—coverage varies significantly by region. Some smaller or independent MLS systems may not be supported by any of these platforms.

Ask about data quality. How recently was the training data updated? Does it cover your specific US market, including rural areas if that's where you work? Stale training data is one of the most common reasons AI predictions underperform. Finally, prioritize tools that offer free demos, trials, or money-back guarantees. Any vendor confident in their product will let you test it first.

## AI Real Estate Trends to Watch in 2025

Generative AI for listing descriptions and marketing copy has moved from experimental to mainstream. Tools built on LLMs now produce property descriptions, social media posts, and email campaigns in seconds. The output quality is strong enough that most consumers can't distinguish AI-generated listings from human-written ones, though experienced agents often still edit for local nuance and brand voice.

AI-powered contract review and compliance tools are emerging fast. Several startups—including Propy and SkySlope's AI features—are building platforms that scan purchase agreements, flag missing disclosures, and check for compliance with state-specific regulations. These tools reduce review time but should not replace legal counsel for complex transactions. Expect major brokerages to adopt these tools by late 2025.

Voice AI and conversational agents for 24/7 lead response are another category gaining traction. Instead of a text-only chatbot, these tools answer phone calls and hold natural conversations with leads—qualifying them, answering property questions, and booking showings without human involvement. Early adoption is promising, though callers sometimes disengage when they realize they're speaking with AI, particularly for high-stakes conversations like pricing discussions.

Following the 2024 NAR settlement and its resulting rule changes around commission transparency and data sharing, MLS organizations are increasingly partnering with AI platforms to share richer data feeds. This is enabling hyperlocal market prediction models that forecast price movements at the neighborhood level with improving accuracy (Source: T3 Sixty, 2025). Keep an eye on [predictive analytics in real estate](/predictive-analytics-real-estate) as these models mature.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best AI software for real estate agents in 2025?
Lofty (formerly Chime) is the top pick for agents and teams who need an AI-powered CRM with lead scoring and automated follow-up. For predictive seller leads specifically, Offrs is a strong alternative. Your best choice depends on whether you need a full CRM or targeted lead generation—check our [real estate lead generation software](/real-estate-lead-generation-software) guide for more options.

### Can AI really predict which homeowners will sell?
Yes, with meaningful accuracy. Tools like Offrs and SmartZip analyze 250–300+ data signals—life events, equity levels, ownership duration—to score homeowners likely to list within 12 months. No tool is perfect, and prediction accuracy varies by market density, but these tools typically outperform cold prospecting by a wide margin. Think of them as a prioritization layer, not a crystal ball.

### How much does AI real estate software cost?
Costs range widely as of Q1 2025. PropStream starts at $99/month for investors. Agent CRMs like Lofty run $400–$700/month for teams. Enterprise platforms like HouseCanary and Reonomy require custom quotes that can run into thousands per month depending on usage volume.

### Is AI real estate software worth it for solo agents?
It depends on your budget and lead volume. PropStream and Offrs offer entry-level pricing that can pencil out for solo agents doing at least a few transactions per quarter. Avoid enterprise tools priced for teams unless you have the volume to justify the cost. A useful benchmark: if the monthly subscription exceeds 5% of your average monthly commission income, you likely need higher volume before the tool pays for itself.

### Does AI real estate software integrate with the MLS?
Most top platforms—Lofty, BoomTown, Follow Up Boss—offer MLS integrations for listing data syncing. Always confirm your specific MLS board is supported before signing up, as coverage varies by region. Some smaller or independent MLS systems may not be supported.

### What's the difference between an AVM and an AI valuation tool?
An AVM (automated valuation model) uses statistical modeling to estimate property value based on comparable sales and property data. AI valuation tools like HouseCanary layer machine learning on top to improve accuracy, factor in property condition data from images, and forecast future value trends—not just estimate current worth. The practical difference: a traditional AVM gives you a snapshot, while an AI-enhanced tool gives you a snapshot plus a directional forecast.
